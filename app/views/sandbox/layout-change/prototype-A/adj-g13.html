The law around making changes

Before you decide that there’s nothing you can do for your employee, be careful that you are not discriminating against them.

! Warning If your employee has a disability, you must make reasonable adjustments if they would otherwise be substantially disadvantaged in doing their job.

The law defines disability as something physical or mental which has a ‘substantial’ and ‘long-term’ effect on someone’s ability to do normal daily activities.

For more, see the definition of disability under the Equality Act 2010 (opens in new tab).

You can find out more on the law around workplace adjustments on the HSE website (opens in new tab)

Unsure if you can make the changes your employee needs

Make any changes you can to help your employee. They can often be temporary and more affordable than you might expect.

Some changes could seem small to you, but they could make a big difference to your employee.
