What to do if your employee has not told you about a health condition or disability

If your employee is off work because of a health condition or disability, they might find it difficult to discuss the details.

It's important to create a culture where employees are happy to talk about their health.

Avoid making judgements when you talk about health conditions or disabilities. This is especially important for mental health conditions.

Not all employees who meet the definition of disabled will think of themselves as a ‘disabled person’. If your employee has not told you that they have a disability, you should consider anything which suggests that they may be disabled and need support.

Check what is classed as a disability under the Equality Act 2010 on GOV.UK (opens in new tab)

Reassuring employees about the safety of the information they disclose

Always make sure you are following GDPR guidelines (opens in a new tab).

Explain to your employee that their health information will be kept private and only used to make changes that will help them do their job or return to work.

You can find out more about the GDPR on GOV.UK (opens in new tab).
