Talking about the condition

It’s important to talk to {{ data['employeeNameC']|default('your employee', true) }} as early as you can.

There’s no perfect way to do it, but it is important to be open to suggestions and let {{ data['employeeNameC']|default('your employee', true) }} tell you what would help. Start with how {{ data['employeeNameC']|default('your employee', true) }} is generally, then move on to work.

You can ask things like:

How is their condition affecting them?

The first step is to talk about how then condition affects them. Physical conditions can affect people in many different ways.

Examples of how a physical condition might affect someone include:

  • if they can get to work, such as if driving is difficult
  • how they can do their job, if sitting for a long time is harder
  • the kind of work they can do, if lifting or carrying causes pain

Will they stay at work or take sick leave?

Staying at work can be good for many people, sometimes with changes to their job. Others will need to take time off.

You can ask {{ data['employeeNameC']|default('your employee', true) }} if they think they would benefit from staying at work, or if they need some sick leave at first.

If you need advice, you can get help from an occupational health expert. They can help your business to maintain a healthy workforce.

  • Taking sick leave - GOV.UK
  • Statutory Sick Pay (SSP) - GOV.UK
  • Using occupational health at work - Acas

Are there any changes at work that would help?

Changes could be needed to help {{ data['employeeNameC']|default('your employee', true) }} stay at work. Things like reduced hours, different shifts or avoiding some duties can help.

You should ask {{ data['employeeNameC']|default('your employee', true) }} about changes that would help them. You can make suggestions, but it’s important to let them take the lead.

  • Reasonable adjustments for workers with disabilities or health conditions - GOV.UK
  • Examples of reasonable adjustments in practice | Equality and Human Rights Commission
