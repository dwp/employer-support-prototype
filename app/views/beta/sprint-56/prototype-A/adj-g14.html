This section: Managing a difficult situation

Options you can consider

Health conditions and disabilities can change over time. Treat each employee's needs individually. An employee with an undiagnosed condition will have very different needs to one managing chronic pain, for example.

Areas to consider supporting your employee

To meet your legal obligations you need to have considered or put into practice as many of the following as possible:

  • discussed what your employee finds difficult and manageable
  • had regular discussions together about what is and is not working
  • put reasonable adjustments in place and work with a Reasonable Adjustment Plan/health passport (opens in new tab)
  • contacted organisations for independent advice or support, such as Acas, HSE or Equality Advisory and Support Services (EASS)
  • let your employee know about possible financial help available to them
  • considered legal representation/advice if a situation has become difficult

If the situation has become difficult to manage

You could consider legal guidance or advice if a situation has become difficult.
